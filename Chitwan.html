Unveil Nepal's Wild Beauty: Embark on a Chitwan Jungle Safari

Nepal beckons adventurers from India with its mesmerizing landscapes and thrilling wildlife encounters. With our Nepal Package Tour, you'll discover a realm where passports are unnecessary, but the adventures are boundless. Nestled in the southwestern expanse lies Chitwan, a district boasting dense forests and untamed wilderness, inviting wildlife enthusiasts to explore its hidden treasures.

Chitwan: The Heart of the Forest:Enveloped by the Siwalik and Mahabharat Ranges, Chitwan Valley pulsates with the rhythm of nature. Its dense forests, once roamed by fierce leopards, now offer sanctuary to diverse wildlife, including the majestic Bengal Tiger and elusive leopard. The name "Chitwan" itself, derived from Sanskrit, embodies its essence—the heart of the forest.

Chitwan National Park: A Wildlife WonderlandAt the heart of Chitwan lies the renowned Chitwan National Park and Parsa Wildlife Reserve, where safari adventures unveil the rich tapestry of Nepal's wildlife. Traverse through lush greens and woodland paths as you seek glimpses of rhinos, mugger crocodiles, Indian rock pythons, and an array of deer species, each contributing to the park's vibrant ecosystem.

Birdwatcher's Paradise: Bishajari TalFor avid birdwatchers, Bishajari Tal, or Lake, offers a tranquil haven teeming with avian wonders. Capture stunning shots of diverse bird species against the backdrop of serene waters, while keeping an eye out for the occasional crocodile basking along the lake's banks.

Riverside Revelations: Rapti River and Narayani BridgeAs the Rapti River meanders through the northern border of Chitwan National Park, it unveils a picturesque spectacle. Cross the Narayani Bridge for panoramic vistas of Nepal's deepest river, where the tranquil flow beckons travelers to explore its banks and nearby islands, offering a perfect setting for a leisurely picnic amidst nature's embrace.

Craft Your Wild ExpeditionJoin us on a Chitwan Jungle Safari, where every moment is infused with the thrill of encountering Nepal's untamed wilderness. Let LIS Exports guide you on an expedition brimming with excitement, adventure, and unforgettable memories amidst the heart of Nepal's forests.

Top things to do in Chitwan National Park, Nepal

  • Jungle Safari:

    Embark on a thrilling jungle safari, either by jeep or on foot, to explore the diverse terrain and wildlife of Chitwan National Park.

  • Elephant Jungle Walks:

    Take a tranquil stroll through the jungle accompanied by majestic elephants, offering a unique perspective on the natural surroundings and wildlife.

  • Bird Watching:

    Join a bird watching tour to marvel at the incredible array of bird species in Nepal, with an impressive count of 848 species to discover.

  • River Safari by Wooden Canoe:

    Experience the beauty of Chitwan on a serene river safari aboard a traditional wooden canoe, where you can spot crocodiles, gharials, and other fascinating aquatic creatures along the Narayani and Rapti rivers.

  • Explore Local Villages

    Immerse yourself in the rich culture and simple traditional way of life by visiting the charming local villages scattered throughout Chitwan.

  • Elephant River Walk and Bathing:

    Witness the enchanting sight of elephants walking and bathing along the banks of the Narayani River, offering a rare and intimate glimpse into their daily lives.

  • Cultural Program:

    Enjoy an evening of captivating cultural performances by the Tharu community, showcasing their vibrant traditions and heritage, right at your Jungle Safari Lodge.

  • Crocodile Breeding Center:

    Gain insight into conservation efforts and learn about the rehabilitation of endangered crocodiles at the Crocodile Breeding Center within Chitwan National Park.

  • Ox Cart Ride:

    Experience the rustic charm of a traditional Ox Cart ride, providing a unique opportunity to explore the local Tharu village community and its surroundings.

  • 20 Thousand Lake Tour:

    Delve into the wonders of birdwatching at the renowned 20 Thousand Lake, also known as 20 Hajar Tal, where you can observe an abundance of avian species thriving in their natural habitat.
