Pelling

Pelling, often referred to as the "Little princess of Nature's kingdom in West Sikkim," is indeed a mesmerizing gem nestled in the lap of the Himalayas. At an altitude of over 7000 feet, this quaint hill town offers a captivating panorama of the majestic Kanchenjunga, seemingly close enough to engage in conversation with its snow-capped peaks. The daybreak in Pelling is a spectacle in itself, with the snowy crowns of the mountains transitioning through hues of pink, gold, and white, while the sunset paints the sky with fiery shades of orange, creating an indescribable ambiance.

But Pelling is not merely about the breathtaking views of Kanchenjunga; it beckons travelers with a plethora of opportunities to explore and immerse themselves in the lap of Mother Nature. Besides the awe-inspiring Kanchenjunga Falls, Rimbi Falls, and Changey Waterfalls, there are other hidden treasures awaiting discovery. The Rock Garden offers a tranquil retreat amidst nature's bountiful beauty, while the Sinsore Bridge provides a picturesque spot to pause and admire the serene surroundings.

The mystical Khechipelri Lake, revered as a sacred site by Buddhists, exudes an aura of tranquility and holiness, inviting visitors to contemplate and find solace in its serene waters. A journey through a pine tree-laden path leads to the lake's tranquil shores, where a small wooden bridge offers a serene vantage point to immerse oneself in the peaceful ambiance. It's a place where weary eyes and restless souls find the respite they seek, a sanctuary where nature's whispers soothe the spirit.

Moreover, the Pemyangse Monastery and the Rabdantse Ruins add a touch of mystique and historical significance to Pelling's charm, offering glimpses into the region's rich cultural and spiritual heritage. Whether it's marveling at the sheer power of cascading waterfalls, seeking blessings at sacred sites, or simply losing oneself in the tranquility of nature, Pelling promises an unforgettable experience for every traveler willing to embrace its enchanting beauty.

Places to visit in pelling

Pemyangse Monastery:The Pemyangse Monastery is a major attraction, not only because of its religious importance but also its history. The monastery that stands at an altitude of over 6,800 feet, is among the oldest in Sikkim. The monastery dates back to the 17th century and is the creation of Lama Lhatsun Chempo.

Pema Yangtse:Pema Yangtse is also an important spot for the trekkers since it is the starting point for the famous Dzongri trek route.

Sangachoeling Monastery:Another famous monastery in the region is the Sangachoeling Monastery, which is just 3 kilometers from Pelling. Absolute silence prevails here and the view of the peaks is simply breathtaking

Singshore Bridge:Another must-visit spot around Pelling is the Singshore Bridge. The 198 meters long suspended structure is the second highest gorge bridge in Asia. The amazing structure between two gorges and offers a birds-eye view of the gorges, which is stunning and quite thrilling. Singshore Bridge just 5 kilometers from Pelling and could be reached within half an hour.

Kaluk:Situated close to Gyalshing, the capital of West Sikkim, Kaluk is a serene village from where one can enjoy a great view of the Kanchenjungha. Lush greeneries and simplicity of villagers are the real charms of the place.
