Gnathang

Nathang Valley, often referred to as the "Ladakh of the East India," is a picturesque gem nestled at an altitude of 13,000 ft. in the Himalayas. Its stunning landscape, surrounded by snow-capped peaks, earns it a well-deserved reputation as one of the most beautiful destinations along the Silk Route Package Tour.

The valley offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Its remote location adds to its allure, making it a haven for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. One of the highlights of Nathang Valley is its proximity to the Chinese Watch Towers on Jelep La, which are visible to the naked eye, adding a touch of intrigue to the scenery.

In recent years, Nathang Valley has emerged as a burgeoning ski destination, drawing adventure enthusiasts from far and wide. Its development as a ski resort, coupled with its accessibility from Calcutta, has transformed it into a sought-after destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

Despite its remoteness, Nathang Valley doesn't lack in modern comforts. Local villagers, with aid from the government, have established homestays equipped with modern facilities to cater to tourists. These accommodations offer a unique opportunity to immerse oneself in the local culture while enjoying the comforts of home.

Throughout the year, Nathang Valley undergoes a remarkable transformation, each season painting the landscape in different hues. From golden hues in autumn to vibrant blooms in the rainy season and a pristine blanket of snow in winter, the valley offers a visual feast for visitors year-round.

The valley's rich biodiversity is further accentuated by the presence of migratory birds, adding to its charm. Additionally, Nathang Valley boasts cultural landmarks such as monasteries, temples, and a war memorial commemorating the "Tukla War," adding depth to its historical significance.

Culinary experiences in Nathang Valley are equally memorable, with home-cooked meals showcasing local flavors. From traditional Sikkimese dishes to comforting favorites like chapatis and puri-sabjis, visitors can savor a taste of the region's culinary heritage.

In essence, Nathang Valley stands as a testament to the raw beauty and cultural richness of the Himalayas, offering a truly unforgettable experience for those willing to venture off the beaten path.

  • Clothing & Luggage

    For those embarking on a journey to Gnathang Valley, it's essential to travel as lightly as possible while ensuring you have the minimum necessary clothing, toiletries, and essentials. Around Gnatnang

Thambi Viewpoint:Situated near Zuluk, Thambi Viewpoint offers a mesmerizing perspective of the famed 95-turn zigzag road, a unique feature in the entire Himalayan range. Perched at an altitude of 11,200 ft, this viewpoint also treats visitors to breathtaking sunrise vistas of majestic Mt. Kanchenjunga.

Kupup Lake:At an elevation of 14,000 ft, Kupup Lake, also known as Elephant Lake due to its distinctive shape resembling an elephant, beckons travelers with its serene beauty. Highlights include the Ice Hockey Ground, the Yak Golf Course (the highest in the world), and hosting the highest water polo game globally. From Kupup, adventurous souls can embark on a journey to Jelep La Pass.

Adi Baba Mandir (Old Baba Mandir):A revered shrine dedicated to Sepoy Baba Harbhajan, who met his demise while on duty, the Adi Baba Mandir stands as a symbol of reverence and remembrance. Located 60 km from Gangtok, this sacred site is cherished by locals and army personnel alike, encapsulating the spirit and memories of Baba Harbhajan Singh.

Menmecho Lake:Nestled in the northeastern region of Sikkim, Menmecho Lake is a tranquil oasis accessible with just an hour's drive from Gnathang. Cradled amidst the Jelep La mountains, it serves as the pristine source of the Rongpo-Chu River, which later converges with the Teesta River as Rongpo. Fed by the melting snow from streams beneath Jelep La, this enchanting lake, located 60 km from Gangtok, exudes serenity and natural splendor. Embark on a journey of discovery and wonder as you explore these captivating destinations surrounding Gnathang Valley, each offering its ownunique charm and allure.
